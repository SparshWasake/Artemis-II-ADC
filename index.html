About The Event

The NASA App Development Challenge is a challenge to design an app using code. You can utilize certain techniques, using data provided by NASA. Our team chose to use Unity(and any other languages needed) for our 3D rendering, Bootstrap(HTML/CSS/JS) for our website, and used Python for creating the actual app.

Frequently Asked Questions

Some questions that people ask us

How do you understand all of this?

It's like learning a new language. At first, it may seem weird and foreign. But as you use it more, it becones natural to you.

What Coding Languages are you using?

We are using HTML, CSS, JS, AOS, and PHP(disabled) for our website. Additionally, we're using Python for our app, and whatever languages you need to use for Unity Engine(for 3D renderings)

How long is the competition?

The competition is 10 weeks or 70 days.

How much money do you get if you win?

Although we don't get any money if we win, we DO get to fly to the Houston Space Center, and get stuff there, and NASA pays for most of the fees!

Why the video?

The video submission is actually pretty important. What it does is explain every attribute of what we've done, what lnaguages we've used, all the info. We also have to display our app there. NASA goes by a rubric to grade this video. If we get a high enough score, we could win.
